Is Slytherin House bad?

Slytherin isn't inherently bad. Its members value ambition, cunning, and resourcefulness. However, individual choices define character, and stereotypes can oversimplify. Slytherin's traits, when wielded positively, contribute to a diverse and balanced wizarding society

Dumbledore, biased?

Dumbledore's bias towards Gryffindor House in Hogwarts is evident in his admiration for bravery and courage. While he values these traits, his favoritism occasionally overlooks the strengths of other houses, highlighting a nuanced aspect of his character

What if Peter Pettigrew didn't betray the Potters?

If Peter Pettigrew hadn't betrayed the Potters, Voldemort's initial downfall might have been averted. Without the unintended assistance, the prophecy concerning Harry might remain incomplete, allowing Voldemort to consolidate power and impose his reign of terror, leading to a world dominated by the Dark Arts
